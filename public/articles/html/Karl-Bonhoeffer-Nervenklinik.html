Karl-Bonhoeffer-Nervenklinik is a railway station in the Reinickendorf borough of Berlin, Germany. It is served by the Berlin S-Bahn, and the U8 line of the U-Bahn

It is named after the adjacent psychiatric hospital. The namesake psychiatrist Karl Bonhoeffer (1868–1948) was the father of the resistance fighters Klaus and Dietrich Bonhoeffer.

U-Bahn Station

The station was only opened on September 29, 1994 as part of the U8 northern extension. When construction began in 1987, commissioning was originally planned for 1992, but was delayed due to the reconstruction measures in the Berlin subway network, which were considered to be more urgent, after German reunification.

Like the other three stations that opened at the same time, the underground station was designed by Rainer G. Rümmler. The walls of the platform hall, which is supported by rectangular columns, and the columns themselves are clad with light brown and red clinker bricks, whose arrangement and color are based on the facades of the old clinic buildings (including the current administration building).
