Breitenbachplatz is a Berlin U-Bahn station located in the Dahlem district on the U3.

It opened on 12 October 1913. The station, and the eponymous square, were named after Minister of State Paul von Breitenbach. It was constructed by W.Leitgebel and closed for a few months during 1945 because of World War II. In 2011 the station was renovated to include a new floor and a lift.

During the Second World War, the station remained largely intact. Only the northern access area was damaged and partially rebuilt only simplified. From the original equipment three wooden double benches were preserved.

The station is a protected monument.

The underground station is barrier-free. An elevator was put into operation on 7 October 2010; the construction costs amounted to 340,000 euros. A guidance system is also available.
