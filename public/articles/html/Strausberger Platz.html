Strausberger Platz is a Berlin U-Bahn station located on the U5.

History

The station was designed by A. Grenander and opened in 1930.

During the Second World War, on 7 May 1944, a ceiling breakthrough occurred in the western station area, caused by several bomb hits. Nevertheless, a shuttle service between Alexanderplatz and Strausberger Platz could be maintained. This was discontinued on 23rd April 1945 and was one of the last in Berlin. Just two months later, on June 20, the trains commuted between Schillingstrasse and Friedrichsfelde. Three days later, the operation continued to Alexanderplatz and was replaced by regular circulation from 1st of February 1946.
