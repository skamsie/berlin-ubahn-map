Kurt-Schumacher-Platz is a station on the U6 line of the Berlin U-Bahn.

There is a bus link outside the station connecting Berlin's Tegel International Airport to the U-Bahn network. The station was opened on 3 May 1956 and named after famous German politician Kurt Schumacher.

The station was built by B. Grimmek. The U6 extension between Seestrasse and Kurt-Schumacher-Platz was the first new subway line built after World War II in Berlin. After this station, the U6 trains travel above ground towards Alt-Tegel (except for Borsigwerke and Alt-Tegel stations that are underground).
