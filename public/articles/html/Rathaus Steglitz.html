Rathaus Steglitz meaning Steglitz Town Hall is the name of both a railway station on the Wannsee Railway and a U-Bahn station in the district of Steglitz in Berlin, Germany, which are close together. It is served by the Berlin S-Bahn, Berlin U-Bahn line U9 and numerous local bus lines.

U-Bahn station

The station was opened on September 30, 1974 in connection with the extension of the U9 line from Walther-Schreiber-Platz to Rathaus Steglitz. The station, which is called Rzu in the BVG station directory, has escalators as well as an elevator and is therefore barrier-free.

The Senate Department also had a second tunnel built for a planned U10. From the Bundesallee / Rheinstraße junction through Schloßstraße to Hermann-Ehlers-Platz, a two-story tunnel was built for the U9 and U10. The U10 was originally supposed to lead via Steglitz Clinic (today: Benjamin Franklin campus of the Charité) to Drakestraße (Wiesenbaude). The planning for the U10 enjoyed high priority until 1984, but then lost importance due to the parallel traffic to the S-Bahn and was finally discontinued in 1993.
