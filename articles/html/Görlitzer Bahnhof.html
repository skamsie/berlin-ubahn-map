The Görlitzer Bahnhof underground station is a station on the U1 and U3 lines of the Berlin U-Bahn railway in the Friedrichshain-Kreuzberg borough. The elevated train station is located above the median strip of Skalitzer Straße, east of its intersection with the Oranienstrasse - Wiener Straße. It was originally called Oranienstrasse, but was renamed in 1926 and since then bears the name of the Görlitz station located to the southeast, partially destroyed in World War II and closed on May 30 1951. In the BVG station directory, the station is identified by the abbreviation Gr.

History

The station was built from 1898 to 1901. The planned name was Görlitzer Bahnhof, and Oranien-Wiener-Straße was also considered. It was opened at the same time as the first section of the elevated and underground railway, but on February 18, 1902 under the name Oranienstrasse.

The concept of the station was designed by the office of Siemens & Halske AG. Since February 14, 1926, the station was called Görlitzer Bahnhof (Oranienstraße) after the nearby head station. The name was changed at the request of the passengers. In 1982 the name extension Oranienstrasse was officially deleted, but stuck to every second station sign.

In 1981 the station was renovated.
